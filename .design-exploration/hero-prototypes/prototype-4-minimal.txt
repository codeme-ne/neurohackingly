DESIGN RATIONALE:
Stripped-down, text-focused design that prioritizes message over decoration.
Small circular photo (74px) acts as brand marker, not hero element. Generous
whitespace and typography do all the heavy lifting. Brutalist/utilitarian aesthetic.

KEY CSS CONCEPTS:
- Single column layout (max-width: 640px, margin: 0 auto)
- Massive vertical spacing (padding: 6rem 0)
- Typography scale: H1 (48px), body (20px), generous line-height (1.8)
- Minimal decorative elements: simple underline, arrow symbols
- Photo: tiny circle (74px), positioned top-right or inline with name
- Input field: borderless with bottom border only

PROS:
✓ Fast load, minimal assets required
✓ Maximum focus on message and value proposition
✓ Timeless aesthetic (won't look dated)
✓ Accessibility-first (relies on semantic HTML and clear hierarchy)
✓ Easy to maintain and iterate

CONS:
✗ Low visual impact (may not grab attention)
✗ Requires exceptional copywriting to compensate for lack of visuals
✗ Photo becomes almost invisible (good or bad depending on goal)
✗ May feel cold/impersonal if not paired with warm, conversational copy
✗ Doesn't leverage full visual potential of web medium

ALTERNATIVE VARIATION:
Replace small photo with text-only brand:

  NEUROHACKINGLY
  ───────────────────────────────────────────
  by Lukas Zangerl

This removes the photo entirely and commits fully to text-based branding.
Photo could appear later in "About" section or sidebar.
